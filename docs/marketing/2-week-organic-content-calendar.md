## How to Use This Calendar

Each post entry contains:
- **Hook** — first line/frame. This is everything. If the hook fails, the post dies.
- **Body** — the substance. Keep it tight.
- **CTA** — one link, one action: `on-device.org`
- **Format** — what type of content to produce
- **Notes** — production tips, timing, what to watch for

Post at peak times for EAT (East Africa Time):
- FB/IG: 7–9am, 12–1pm, 7–9pm
- WhatsApp Status: 6–8am (people check phones waking up), 8–10pm
- TikTok: 7–9pm (after work, after dinner)

## WEEK 2: PULL (Deepen trust, handle objections, create urgency)

Week 1 planted seeds. Week 2 harvests them — by addressing the real objections, making the social proof visible, and creating mild urgency without being pushy.

---

**Channel:** FB Feed + IG Feed + WhatsApp Status

**Hook:** "Your phone will say 'Unknown source.' Here's why that's fine."

**Body:**
When you download OnDevice AI, Android shows a warning.

This happens for EVERY app not on the Play Store. It's a blanket message — not a judgment on this specific app.

Here's the truth:
- The app runs entirely on your device
- It sends nothing to the internet after download
- It's signed by OnDevice AI Inc. (Canadian company, incorporated 2026)
- SHA-256 checksum published at on-device.org/install

We're not on the Play Store yet. We're shipping direct. That's the tradeoff. You get it cheaper; we skip 30% Google fees.

on-device.org/install has the full install guide.

**Format:** Text post with clear heading. No images needed — authenticity is the design.

**Notes:** Address this head-on. People are scared of sideloading. The SHA-256 mention is a trust signal even if 99% of readers don't know what it means — it signals you're serious about security. Link to the install page specifically.

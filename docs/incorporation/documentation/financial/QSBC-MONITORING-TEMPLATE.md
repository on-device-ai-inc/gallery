# QSBC ASSET MONITORING SPREADSHEET
## Quarterly Compliance Tracking for LCGE Eligibility

**Purpose:** Ensure corporation maintains Qualified Small Business Corporation (QSBC) status for Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption (LCGE)

**Critical Rule:** Corporation must maintain >50% active business assets throughout 24-month holding period AND >90% at time of sale

---

## QSBC QUALIFICATION TESTS

### Test 1: At Time of Sale (90% Active Assets)
≥90% of corporation's assets (by FMV) must be used principally in active business carried on in Canada

### Test 2: Throughout 24-Month Lookback (50% Active Assets)
>50% of corporation's assets (by FMV) must have been used principally in active business throughout the 24 months immediately before the sale

**If you fail either test:** LCGE is DENIED entirely. No retroactive fixes available.

**Solution:** Monitor quarterly and purify immediately if falling below 60% active assets.

## ASSET CLASSIFICATION GUIDE

### ACTIVE BUSINESS ASSETS (Count Toward Qualification)

**Equipment & Property:**
✅ Computer equipment (DGX Spark, laptops, servers) used in development
✅ Office furniture and equipment actively used
✅ Leasehold improvements for business operations
✅ Vehicles used in business (delivery, client visits)

**Intellectual Property:**
✅ Developed software and applications (OnDevice AI code)
✅ Patents, trademarks related to active business
✅ Work-in-progress development (code being written)

**Working Capital:**
✅ Cash necessary for operations (<3 months operating expenses)
✅ Accounts receivable from customers
✅ Inventory (if applicable)
✅ Prepaid expenses (annual software subscriptions, hosting credits)

**Investments in Active Business:**
✅ Shares in other active Canadian businesses (if <50% ownership)

---

### PASSIVE ASSETS (Count Against Qualification)

**Excess Cash:**
❌ Cash beyond 3-6 months operating expenses
❌ Savings accounts with large balances
❌ Money market funds

**Investments:**
❌ GICs, bonds, term deposits
❌ Public company stocks
❌ Mutual funds, ETFs
❌ Cryptocurrency (unless business is crypto-related)

**Real Estate:**
❌ Rental properties (unless real estate is your business)
❌ Land held for investment
❌ Buildings not used in business

**Loans & Receivables:**
❌ Loans to shareholders (unless repayment imminent)
❌ Loans to related parties
❌ Non-business receivables

**Other:**
❌ Art, collectibles, precious metals
❌ Personal use assets

## WHEN TO PURIFY (Action Triggers)

### Trigger 1: Active % Falls Below 60%
**Action:** Immediate purification required

**Purification strategies:**
1. **Accelerate planned spending:**
   - Purchase equipment needed in next 12 months NOW
   - Prepay annual software subscriptions
   - Stock up on cloud credits
   - Hire contractors for planned work

2. **Pay dividend to holding company:**
   - Removes excess cash
   - Requires holding company structure (advanced)

3. **Repay shareholder loans:**
   - If you loaned corporation money, get repaid
   - Reduces total assets, improves ratio

4. **Invest in business development:**
   - Marketing campaigns (active business expense)
   - R&D projects (SR&ED eligible)
   - Product development costs

**DON'T:**
- Keep excess cash "just in case"
- Park money in GICs or investments
- Delay purification hoping for future revenue

---

### Trigger 2: Revenue Windfall

**Scenario:** OnDevice AI gets $100,000 in sales suddenly

**Problem:**
- Cash balance jumps from $10,000 to $110,000
- Active assets stay same: $30,000
- Passive assets become: $80,000 (excess cash)
- Active %: $30,000 / $110,000 = 27% ❌ **MASSIVE FAIL**

**Solution (within 90 days):**
1. Pay yourself salary: $60,000 (removes cash, creates SR&ED claim)
2. Accelerate planned spending: $20,000 (equipment, services)
3. Pay dividend if holding company: $20,000
4. Result: Cash = $10,000, Active % back to 75%+

**This is why quarterly monitoring matters!**

## VALUATION GUIDELINES

**For quarterly monitoring:**

**Equipment:** Use declining fair market value
- DGX Spark: $6,000 (Year 1) → $4,500 (Year 2) → $3,000 (Year 3)
- Estimate 25% annual depreciation

**Intellectual Property:** Conservative estimate
- Pre-revenue: Minimal value ($1,000-$10,000)
- Post-revenue: Multiple of annual revenue (1-3x)
- Use lower end of range for safety

**Working Capital:** Actual bank balance
- Cash: Actual amount in bank
- Accounts receivable: Actual amounts owed

**Use consistent valuation method each quarter**

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ **"I'll check this at year-end"**
- Too late if you failed in Q2
- 24-month clock resets, delaying LCGE eligibility

❌ **"Cash is for emergencies, I'll keep it"**
- Holding excess cash is the #1 QSBC killer
- Use LOC or shareholder loan for emergencies instead

❌ **"I'll purify when I'm ready to sell"**
- 24-month lookback means you needed 50%+ for past 2 years
- Cannot retroactively fix past quarters

❌ **"My accountant will handle this"**
- Many accountants miss QSBC monitoring until too late
- Owner responsibility to track quarterly

✅ **DO THIS:**
- Monitor every quarter (15 minutes)
- Purify proactively when approaching 60%
- Keep excess cash under 6 months operating expenses
- Document everything

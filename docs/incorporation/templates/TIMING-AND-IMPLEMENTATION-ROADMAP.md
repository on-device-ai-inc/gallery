## The 24-Month QSBC Holding Period Trap

### How the 24-Month Rule Works

**ITA Requirement:** For shares to qualify as QSBC shares eligible for LCGE:

> "Throughout the 24-month period immediately preceding the disposition, more than 50% of the FMV of the corporation's assets were used principally in an active business carried on primarily in Canada."

**Critical Implication:** Each beneficiary must hold the shares (directly or through trust) for **24 continuous months** before they can use LCGE.

**Trust Holding Satisfies Requirement:** Per *Pellerin* (2015 TCC), shares held by a family trust COUNT toward beneficiaries' 24-month holding period.

**BUT:** The trust must have held the shares for 24 months BEFORE the beneficiary can use LCGE on distribution/exit.

### Real-World Impact: The "Rolling Beneficiary" Problem

**Your Family:**
- Child 1 (age 18) - born 2008
- Child 2 (age 16) - born 2010
- Child 3 (age 14) - born 2012
- Child 4 (age 11) - born 2015

**Trust Created:** January 2024
**Exit Event:** December 2026 (36 months later)
**Trust Holding Period:** 36 months ✅

**Can all 4 children use LCGE?**

**Analysis:**
- Child 1 (age 18 at creation, 21 at exit): ✅ YES - Trust held shares for 36 months
- Child 2 (age 16 at creation, 19 at exit): ✅ YES - Trust held shares for 36 months
- Child 3 (age 14 at creation, 17 at exit): ✅ YES - Trust held shares for 36 months
- Child 4 (age 11 at creation, 14 at exit): ✅ YES - Trust held shares for 36 months

**All 4 qualify** because trust holding period satisfies the requirement for ALL beneficiaries.

**Now Compare: Trust Created 18 Months Before Exit (July 2025)**

**Trust Created:** July 2025
**Exit Event:** December 2026 (18 months later)
**Trust Holding Period:** 18 months ❌ (less than 24 months)

**Can any children use LCGE?**

## The GAAR Timing Correlation Test

### How CRA Analyzes Timing for "Primary Purpose" Test

Post-*Deans Knight* (2023 SCC), CRA applies a **"functional equivalence"** test:

**Question:** Does the timing and structure achieve a result that is functionally equivalent to a prohibited outcome?

**For family trusts, CRA examines:**

1. **Temporal Correlation:** When was trust created relative to:
   - First contact from potential acquirers
   - Funding rounds (sudden valuation increases)
   - Business inflection points (major revenue/profit changes)
   - Estate freeze transactions
   - Any "liquidity event" planning

2. **Primary Purpose Analysis:** Was the trust created for:
   - **Business purposes** (income splitting, estate planning, asset protection, succession planning)
   - **Tax purposes** (LCGE multiplication for imminent exit)

3. **Economic Substance:** Does the trust have:
   - Multi-year operational history
   - Distribution history to beneficiaries
   - Investment policy management
   - Independent trustee governance

## ENHANCED RECITAL C (Multi-Purpose - STRONG GAAR Defense)

**REPLACE RECITAL C WITH:**

**C. PURPOSES OF THIS TRUST**

The purposes of this Trust are to achieve the following business, family, and succession planning objectives:

**a) Succession Planning and Business Continuity**

To facilitate the orderly transition of ownership and control of OnDevice AI Inc. to the next generation of the family, ensuring continuity of the business enterprise and preservation of the family's entrepreneurial legacy. This includes:
- Gradual introduction of beneficiaries to business ownership responsibilities
- Maintenance of unified family ownership during founder's lifetime
- Planned transition of voting and economic interests over time
- Preservation of business relationships and goodwill through stable ownership

**b) Asset Protection and Creditor Proofing**

To provide creditor protection for family wealth by holding shares in a separate legal entity (the Trust) distinct from the personal assets of the founder and spouse. This protects the family's business equity from:
- Personal creditors of individual family members
- Matrimonial property claims in the event of marriage breakdown
- Professional liability claims
- Business risks unrelated to the corporation itself

**c) Estate Planning and Probate Avoidance**

To minimize estate administration costs and delays by holding shares outside the founder's estate, thereby:
- Avoiding probate fees on the value of shares (Ontario: 1.5% of estate value over $50,000)
- Ensuring shares pass to beneficiaries without estate administration delay
- Providing liquidity for estate tax obligations without forced sale of business
- Enabling continued business operations during estate settlement

**d) Family Governance and Unified Decision-Making**

To establish a governance framework for family participation in business decisions while maintaining unified ownership during the beneficiaries' minority. This includes:
- Trustee oversight of share voting to ensure business strategy alignment
- Coordinated family approach to major corporate decisions
- Education of next generation in responsible ownership
- Dispute resolution framework for family disagreements

**e) Financial Support and Education Funding**

To provide a mechanism for trustees to support beneficiaries' financial needs, including:
- Educational expenses (tuition, books, living expenses during post-secondary education)
- Health and medical expenses not covered by insurance
- Housing assistance (down payments, rental support)
- Entrepreneurial or career development support
- Support during periods of unemployment or economic hardship

**f) Income Equalization and Tax Efficiency**

To enable income and capital distributions to beneficiaries in a tax-efficient manner that:
- Recognizes the contribution of family members to business success
- Equalizes economic benefits among children despite different earning capacities
- Utilizes beneficiaries' graduated tax brackets and personal exemptions
- Allocates income to beneficiaries who contribute labor or capital to the business

**g) Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption Utilization**

To utilize the Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption for qualifying small business corporation shares as intended by Parliament under subsection 110.6 of the Income Tax Act, enabling:
- Each beneficiary to claim their individual LCGE entitlement
- Tax-free realization of capital gains on qualifying dispositions
- Multiplication of LCGE across multiple family members in accordance with legislative intent
- Preservation of family wealth through tax-efficient exit planning

**h) Flexibility for Changing Circumstances**

To provide trustees with discretion to adapt distributions and planning to changing family and business circumstances, including:
- Varying needs among beneficiaries at different life stages
- Changing tax laws and planning opportunities
- Evolution of business operations and ownership structure
- Unforeseen family events (disability, early death, marriage, divorce)

## PURPOSE HIERARCHY - GAAR Defense

**PRIMARY PURPOSES (Non-Tax):**
1. Succession planning → Business continuity (a)
2. Asset protection → Creditor proofing (b)
3. Estate planning → Probate avoidance (c)
4. Family governance → Unified ownership (d)
5. Beneficiary support → Education/health (e)

**SECONDARY PURPOSES (Tax-Related):**
6. Income equalization → Tax efficiency (f)
7. LCGE utilization → Parliamentary intent (g)
8. Flexibility → Adaptive planning (h)

**GAAR Three-Part Test Analysis:**

1. **Tax benefit obtained?** Yes (LCGE multiplication, income splitting)

2. **Avoidance transaction (no bona fide non-tax purpose)?** **NO**
   - Primary purposes (a-e) are legitimate business and family objectives
   - Tax purposes (f-g) are ONE of EIGHT documented purposes
   - Trust created at inception, not late-stage reorganization
   - Long-term holding contemplated (21+ years)

3. **Abusive tax avoidance?** **NO**
   - LCGE multiplication is Parliament's intent (s. 110.6 allows per-person claims)
   - Income splitting follows TOSI rules (legitimate labor/capital returns)
   - Standard family trust structure (not aggressive or unusual)
   - Substance over form: genuine succession planning, not sham

**Conclusion:** GAAR should NOT apply due to multiple documented business purposes.

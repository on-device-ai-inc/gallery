## Subscription Method for Family Trust - Subsection 75(2) Compliance

**Version:** 1.0

**Purpose:** Provide step-by-step execution instructions for trust to acquire CCPC shares through subscription, ensuring subsection 75(2) compliance

## Why This Protocol Exists

**Critical Finding:** The Ultimate Trust Deed template describes share acquisition options but doesn't prescribe the optimal method or provide execution instructions. This gap creates **subsection 75(2) non-compliance risk** if executed incorrectly.

**The Problem:**
- **Gift from founder** → Triggers subsection 75(2) attribution (property can revert)
- **Purchase below FMV** → Triggers subsection 75(2) attribution (benefit conferred)
- **Unclear documentation** → CRA challenges economic substance in audit

**The Solution:**
✅ **Subscription at authorized capital value** - Clean, defensible, minimal cost

## Step-by-Step Execution Timeline

### Prerequisites (Before Trust Creation)

**1. Determine Share Structure**

Your Articles of Incorporation should authorize multiple share classes:

- **Class A** - Voting common shares (founder retains control)
- **Class B** - Non-voting common shares (trust subscribes for these)
- **Class C** - Non-voting preferred shares (for future VC investors)

**Why Class B for Trust:**
- Founder retains voting control (Class A)
- Trust participates in equity growth (Class B non-voting common)
- VCs can invest without diluting family (Class C preferred)

**2. Calculate Subscription Price**

**Formula:** (Authorized capital) ÷ (Number of shares to be issued)

**Example:**
- Articles authorize 1,000,000 Class B shares at $0.01 par value
- Trust will subscribe for 400 Class B shares
- Subscription price: $0.01 × 400 = **$4.00 total**

**IMPORTANT:** This is NOT the "market value" - this is the **authorized capital value** at which the corporation can issue new shares. There is NO valuation required because the corporation is setting the price for its own shares.

**3. Prepare Trust Deed**

- Use Ultimate Trust Deed template with [PLACEHOLDERS] customized
- Ensure third-party settlor identified (see SETTLOR-SELECTION-GUIDE.md)
- Confirm beneficiary classes include founder's children

## Day 1: Trust Creation

### Step 1: Execute Trust Deed (Morning)

**Who:** Settlor + Initial Trustees (minimum 2 trustees recommended)

**Required Actions:**
1. Settlor signs trust deed
2. Initial trustees sign acceptance of trusteeship
3. Witnesses sign (one witness per signature, independent third parties)
4. Settlor delivers nominal property to trustees ($10 cash or other property)

**Documentation:**
- ✅ Original signed trust deed (all parties)
- ✅ Witness declarations
- ✅ Receipt for nominal property ($10)

**CRITICAL:** The settlor's $10 contribution must be the settlor's own funds (NOT reimbursed by founder). This establishes true third-party status.

### Step 2: Initial Trustee Meeting (Afternoon)

**Who:** Trustees only (settlor NOT present)

**Purpose:** Trustees resolve to subscribe for 400 Class B shares

**Template Resolution:**

```markdown
INITIAL MEETING OF TRUSTEES
[TRUST NAME]
[DATE]

PRESENT:
- [Trustee 1 Name], Trustee
- [Trustee 2 Name], Trustee

SUBSCRIPTION FOR SHARES

WHEREAS:
1. The Trust was settled on [DATE] by [SETTLOR NAME] (the "Settlor");
2. The Trustees have received the Trust Deed and accepted their appointment;
3. The Trustees have determined it is in the best interests of the Beneficiaries to invest Trust property in shares of [CORPORATION NAME] (the "Corporation");
4. The Corporation is a Canadian-controlled private corporation incorporated under the laws of [PROVINCE];
5. The Trustees wish to subscribe for Class B non-voting common shares in the capital of the Corporation;

IT IS RESOLVED THAT:

1. **Subscription Approved:** The Trustees are authorized and directed to subscribe for 400 Class B non-voting common shares in the capital of [CORPORATION NAME] at a subscription price of $0.01 per share, for a total subscription price of $4.00 (the "Subscription");

2. **Source of Funds:** The subscription price of $4.00 shall be paid from Trust property, being $10.00 received from the Settlor on [DATE];

3. **No Reversion:** The Trustees confirm that:
   a) The shares will be held in trust for the Beneficiaries as defined in the Trust Deed;
   b) The Settlor has NO beneficial interest in the shares or any right to receive them back;
   c) The shares will NOT revert to the Settlor under any circumstances;
   d) This subscription is made at the Trustees' discretion for the benefit of the Beneficiaries;

4. **Authorization:** [Trustee 1 Name] is authorized to:
   a) Execute all documents necessary to complete the Subscription;
   b) Deliver the subscription price of $4.00 to the Corporation;
   c) Receive share certificates on behalf of the Trust;
   d) Take all other actions reasonably necessary to complete the Subscription;

5. **Recordkeeping:** The Trustees shall maintain:
   a) Copy of subscription documents
   b) Share certificates
   c) Proof of payment ($4.00)
   d) Correspondence with Corporation
   e) All documents evidencing the arm's-length nature of this transaction;

6. **Independence Confirmation:** The Trustees confirm that:
   a) This decision was made independently based on the best interests of the Beneficiaries;
   b) No direction, suggestion, or influence was received from [FOUNDER NAME] or any other person;
   c) The Trustees exercised their discretion freely and without constraint;

SIGNED this [DATE].

_______________________________
[Trustee 1 Name], Trustee


_______________________________
[Trustee 2 Name], Trustee
```

**Documentation:**
- ✅ Signed trustee resolution (both trustees)
- ✅ Meeting minutes
- ✅ Copy retained in trust records

## Day 2-3: Corporation Accepts Subscription

### Step 3: Corporation Board Meeting

**Who:** Corporation's board of directors (typically the founder as sole director initially)

**Purpose:** Accept trust's subscription and authorize share issuance

**Template Resolution:**

```markdown
MEETING OF DIRECTORS
[CORPORATION NAME]
[DATE]

PRESENT:
- [FOUNDER NAME], Director

SHARE ISSUANCE TO [TRUST NAME]

WHEREAS:
1. The Corporation is authorized to issue an unlimited number of Class B non-voting common shares pursuant to its Articles of Incorporation;
2. The Trustees of [TRUST NAME] (the "Trust") have subscribed for 400 Class B non-voting common shares at a price of $0.01 per share, being a total subscription price of $4.00;
3. The subscription price represents the authorized capital value of the shares;
4. The Board deems it in the best interests of the Corporation to accept this subscription;

IT IS RESOLVED THAT:

1. **Subscription Accepted:** The subscription by the Trust for 400 Class B non-voting common shares at $0.01 per share is hereby accepted;

2. **Share Issuance Authorized:** The Corporation is authorized and directed to:
   a) Issue 400 Class B non-voting common shares to the Trust;
   b) Receive payment of $4.00 from the Trust;
   c) Issue share certificate(s) in the name of the Trustees of [TRUST NAME];

3. **Share Certificate:** Share certificate number CB-001 shall be issued as follows:
   - **Registered Owner:** [Trustee 1 Name] and [Trustee 2 Name], as Trustees of [TRUST NAME]
   - **Share Class:** Class B non-voting common shares
   - **Number of Shares:** 400
   - **Consideration:** $4.00 cash (PAID IN FULL)

4. **Securities Ledger:** The Corporation's securities ledger shall be updated to reflect:
   - Issuance of 400 Class B shares
   - Registered owner: Trustees of [TRUST NAME]
   - Date of issuance: [DATE]
   - Consideration: $4.00 cash

5. **Recordkeeping:** The Corporation shall maintain in its minute book:
   - Copy of trust subscription resolution
   - Proof of payment received ($4.00)
   - Original share certificate stub
   - Updated securities ledger

6. **Independence Confirmation:** The Board confirms that:
   a) This issuance is an arm's-length transaction;
   b) The subscription price ($4.00) represents the authorized capital value;
   c) No special consideration or benefit is being conferred on the Trust;
   d) The Trust is subscribing on the same terms available to any other subscriber;

SIGNED this [DATE].

_______________________________
[FOUNDER NAME], Director
```

**Documentation:**
- ✅ Signed director resolution
- ✅ Meeting minutes
- ✅ Updated share register

### Step 4: Payment and Share Certificate Issuance

**Action Items:**

1. **Trust pays $4.00 to Corporation**
   - Method: Trustee writes cheque or bank transfer from trust bank account
   - Memo: "Subscription for 400 Class B shares - [Trust Name]"
   - Documentation: Retain copy of cheque/transfer confirmation

2. **Corporation issues share certificate**
   - Certificate number: CB-001 (Class B, certificate #1)
   - Registered owner: "[Trustee 1 Name] and [Trustee 2 Name], as Trustees of [TRUST NAME]"
   - Number of shares: 400
   - Class: Class B non-voting common shares
   - Date of issue: [DATE]
   - Signed by: Corporate officers (President/Secretary)

3. **Corporation updates securities ledger**
   - Record 400 Class B shares issued to Trust
   - Record consideration received: $4.00 cash
   - Date: [DATE]

**Documentation:**
- ✅ Share certificate CB-001 (original to trustees, copy in minute book)
- ✅ Proof of payment ($4.00 - bank record)
- ✅ Updated securities ledger

## Common Errors to Avoid

### 🔴 FATAL ERROR #1: Gift Instead of Subscription

**Wrong:**
- Founder transfers existing Class A shares to trust as gift
- Trust receives shares with no payment

**Why Fatal:**
- Subsection 75(2) applies (property can revert to founder)
- Section 107(4.1) denies rollover treatment forever
- LCGE benefits destroyed

**Correct:**
- Trust subscribes for NEW Class B shares
- Trust pays $4.00 cash consideration
- Founder never owned the Class B shares (cannot revert)

---

### 🔴 FATAL ERROR #2: Below-FMV Purchase

**Wrong:**
- Trust purchases shares from founder for $100
- Shares actually worth $50,000 (recent valuation)
- Trust got a "deal"

**Why Fatal:**
- Benefit conferred = subsection 75(2) trigger
- CRA will argue underpayment = gift element
- Attribution applies

**Correct:**
- Trust subscribes for shares at authorized capital ($4)
- OR Trust purchases at TRUE FMV with independent valuation ($50,000)
- No benefit conferred

---

### 🔴 FATAL ERROR #3: Founder Directs Trustees

**Wrong:**
- Founder emails trustees: "Here's the share subscription form, please sign"
- Trustees sign without independent decision-making
- CRA interviews trustees 5 years later: "Did you decide to subscribe independently?" Trustees: "Well, [Founder] told us to..."

**Why Fatal:**
- No true trustee discretion = sham transaction
- Subsection 75(2) argues property held for founder's benefit
- GAAR risk (functional equivalence to founder holding shares)

**Correct:**
- Trustees meet independently (founder NOT present)
- Trustees document their analysis of investment in corporate minutes
- Trustees make independent decision to subscribe
- Trustees can articulate their reasoning under CRA audit interview

---

### ⚠️ ERROR #4: Poor Documentation

**Wrong:**
- Verbal agreement that trust will subscribe
- No written trustee resolutions
- Handwritten share certificate
- No proof of $4 payment

**Why Problematic:**
- CRA auditors demand contemporaneous documentation
- Lack of evidence = CRA fills gaps with adverse assumptions
- Can't reconstruct dates/consideration years later

**Correct:**
- All resolutions dated, signed, witnessed
- Professional share certificate with corporate seal
- Bank records proving $4 payment
- Securities ledger updated contemporaneously
- All documents in corporate minute book + trust records

## Alternative Method: Purchase at FMV (If Shares Already Issued)

**When to Use:**
- Corporation already has existing shareholders
- Founder already holds all shares
- Trust creation is 2-5 years after incorporation

**Requirements:**
1. **Independent Valuation:** Engage qualified business valuator ($10K-$15K)
2. **FMV Payment:** Trust pays TRUE fair market value (no discounts)
3. **Legal Opinion:** Lawyer confirms subsection 75(2) compliance
4. **Documentation:** Purchase agreement, valuation report, proof of payment

**Advantages:**
- Still avoids subsection 75(2) (per *Sommerer* precedent - FMV sales are safe)
- Can be done post-incorporation

**Disadvantages:**
- Expensive ($10K-$15K valuation cost)
- Requires trust to have significant funds (FMV could be $50K-$500K+)
- PUC grinding issues if founder's shares had high ACB
- More complex CRA audit defense

**Win-Probability:** 85% (vs. 95% for subscription)

**Recommendation:** Only use this if subscription is impossible (shares already issued and can't create new class).

## CRA Audit Interview - Trustee Preparation

**If CRA interviews trustees 5 years later, they should be able to say:**

**Q: "Why did the trust subscribe for shares in [Corporation]?"**
**A:** "We reviewed the corporation's business plan and growth prospects. As trustees, we determined that investing trust property in a Canadian-controlled private corporation with strong growth potential was in the best long-term interests of the beneficiaries. The subscription allowed the trust to participate in the corporation's equity growth."

**Q: "Who decided that the trust would subscribe?"**
**A:** "The trustees made that decision at our initial meeting on [DATE]. We met independently, reviewed the opportunity, and resolved to subscribe for 400 Class B shares."

**Q: "Did [Founder] direct you to subscribe or suggest it?"**
**A:** "No. [Founder] is not involved in trust decisions. The trustees exercise independent discretion based on the best interests of the beneficiaries."

**Q: "How much did the trust pay for the shares?"**
**A:** "We subscribed for 400 Class B shares at the authorized capital value of $0.01 per share, paying $4.00 total in cash from trust property."

**Q: "Where did the $4 come from?"**
**A:** "From the trust's initial property. The settlor contributed $10 when the trust was created. The trustees used $4 of that to subscribe for shares."

**Q: "Can the shares ever go back to [Founder] or the settlor?"**
**A:** "No. The shares are held in trust for the beneficiaries - [Founder]'s children. The settlor has no beneficial interest and the trust deed prohibits any reversion to the settlor."

**These answers demonstrate:**
- ✅ Independent trustee decision-making
- ✅ Business purpose analysis
- ✅ Arm's-length consideration
- ✅ No reversionary interest
- ✅ Subsection 75(2) compliance

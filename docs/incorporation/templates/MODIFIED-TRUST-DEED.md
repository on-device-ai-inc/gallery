## RECITALS

**WHEREAS:**

A. The Settlor wishes to create a discretionary family trust for the benefit of the Beneficiaries (as defined below);

B. The Trustees have agreed to act as trustees of this Trust and to hold the Trust Property upon the trusts and subject to the powers and provisions herein contained;

**C. PURPOSES OF THIS TRUST (ENHANCED - GAAR DEFENSE)**

The purposes of this Trust are to achieve the following business, family, and succession planning objectives:

**a) Succession Planning and Business Continuity**

To facilitate the orderly transition of ownership and control of [CORPORATION NAME] to the next generation of the family, ensuring continuity of the business enterprise and preservation of the family's entrepreneurial legacy. This includes:
- Gradual introduction of beneficiaries to business ownership responsibilities
- Maintenance of unified family ownership during founder's lifetime
- Planned transition of voting and economic interests over time
- Preservation of business relationships and goodwill through stable ownership

**b) Asset Protection and Creditor Proofing**

To provide creditor protection for family wealth by holding shares in a separate legal entity (the Trust) distinct from the personal assets of the founder and spouse. This protects the family's business equity from:
- Personal creditors of individual family members
- Matrimonial property claims in the event of marriage breakdown
- Professional liability claims
- Business risks unrelated to the corporation itself

**c) Estate Planning and Probate Avoidance**

To minimize estate administration costs and delays by holding shares outside the founder's estate, thereby:
- Avoiding probate fees on the value of shares (Ontario: 1.5% of estate value over $50,000)
- Ensuring shares pass to beneficiaries without estate administration delay
- Providing liquidity for estate tax obligations without forced sale of business
- Enabling continued business operations during estate settlement

**d) Family Governance and Unified Decision-Making**

To establish a governance framework for family participation in business decisions while maintaining unified ownership during the beneficiaries' minority. This includes:
- Trustee oversight of share voting to ensure business strategy alignment
- Coordinated family approach to major corporate decisions
- Education of next generation in responsible ownership
- Dispute resolution framework for family disagreements

**e) Financial Support and Education Funding**

To provide a mechanism for trustees to support beneficiaries' financial needs, including:
- Educational expenses (tuition, books, living expenses during post-secondary education)
- Health and medical expenses not covered by insurance
- Housing assistance (down payments, rental support)
- Entrepreneurial or career development support
- Support during periods of unemployment or economic hardship

**f) Income Equalization and Tax Efficiency**

To enable income and capital distributions to beneficiaries in a tax-efficient manner that:
- Recognizes the contribution of family members to business success
- Equalizes economic benefits among children despite different earning capacities
- Utilizes beneficiaries' graduated tax brackets and personal exemptions
- Allocates income to beneficiaries who contribute labor or capital to the business

**g) Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption Utilization**

To utilize the Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption for qualifying small business corporation shares as intended by Parliament under subsection 110.6 of the Income Tax Act, enabling:
- Each beneficiary to claim their individual LCGE entitlement
- Tax-free realization of capital gains on qualifying dispositions
- Multiplication of LCGE across multiple family members in accordance with legislative intent
- Preservation of family wealth through tax-efficient exit planning

**h) Flexibility for Changing Circumstances**

To provide trustees with discretion to adapt distributions and planning to changing family and business circumstances, including:
- Varying needs among beneficiaries at different life stages
- Changing tax laws and planning opportunities
- Evolution of business operations and ownership structure
- Unforeseen family events (disability, early death, marriage, divorce)

D. This Trust is intended to avoid application of subsection 75(2) of the Income Tax Act (Canada) and to preserve rollover treatment under Section 107(2).

## 5. TRUSTEES' POWERS AND DUTIES

### 5.1 Discretionary Powers

The Trustees shall have FULL DISCRETION to:

a) **Allocate Income:** Determine which Beneficiaries (if any) shall receive income distributions and in what amounts;

b) **Allocate Capital:** Determine which Beneficiaries (if any) shall receive capital distributions and in what amounts;

c) **Retain Income:** Retain income within the Trust for reinvestment or future distribution;

d) **Investment:** Invest and reinvest Trust Property in any form of property, real or personal, without restriction;

e) **Shares:** Hold, vote, and exercise all rights attaching to shares held by the Trust;

f) **Borrow:** Borrow money and secure such borrowing against Trust Property;

g) **Professional Advice:** Engage accountants, lawyers, investment advisors, and other professionals;

**h) Decision-Making (MODIFIED):** Where there are multiple Trustees:

  **(i) Unanimous agreement required for:**
  - Distributions exceeding $50,000 in any calendar year
  - Disposition of shares held by the Trust
  - Termination of the Trust
  - Amendment of trust deed provisions (subject to Article 8.5 restrictions)
  - Borrowing exceeding $25,000
  - Engagement of professionals for fees exceeding $10,000

  **(ii) Majority agreement sufficient for:**
  - Routine administrative matters
  - Engagement of professionals within approved budget
  - Investment decisions within approved investment policy
  - Income and capital distributions under $50,000
  - Annual tax elections and filings

### 5.2 Standard of Care

The Trustees shall exercise the care, diligence, and skill that a reasonably prudent person would exercise in comparable circumstances.

### 5.3 No Compensation

The Trustees shall serve without compensation unless unanimously agreed otherwise. The Trustees may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in administering the Trust.

### 5.4 Trustee Resignation and Replacement

a) Any Trustee may resign by giving 30 days' written notice to the other Trustees and Beneficiaries;

b) A Trustee may be replaced by unanimous decision of the remaining Trustees;

c) If no Trustees remain, the Beneficiaries (if of legal age) may appoint a replacement Trustee.

### **5.6 Trustee Indemnification (NEW)**

**a) Indemnification**

The Trustees shall be indemnified from the Trust Property against all costs, charges, and expenses reasonably incurred in the administration of the Trust, except for:
  (i) Losses arising from trustee's willful misconduct or gross negligence;
  (ii) Losses arising from breach of trust or conflict of interest;
  (iii) Personal fines or penalties imposed for violation of law.

**b) Limitation of Liability**

The Trustees shall not be personally liable for:
  (i) Errors of judgment made in good faith;
  (ii) Depreciation in value of trust investments;
  (iii) Acts or omissions of agents, advisors, or professionals reasonably selected;
  (iv) Any loss not resulting from their own willful default or gross negligence;
  (v) Tax liabilities of the Trust (which are obligations of the Trust, not Trustees personally).

**c) Insurance**

The Trustees may, at the expense of the Trust, obtain and maintain insurance coverage to protect against liability arising from their role as Trustees.

### 6.3 Section 107(2) Rollover Preservation

**CRITICAL FOR TAX PLANNING:**

Distributions of Trust Property (including shares) to Beneficiaries may qualify for tax-deferred rollover treatment under subsection 107(2) of the Income Tax Act, provided:

a) The distribution is made before the 21-year deemed disposition date;
b) The Beneficiary was resident in Canada immediately before the distribution;
c) No subsection 75(2) attribution applies;
d) The Trust elects rollover treatment if required.

**The Trustees should consider using Section 107(2) rollover in the following circumstances:**
- Years 19-20 of the Trust (before 21-year deemed disposition)
- When beneficiaries reach age 18+ and can hold shares directly
- Before anticipated sale of corporation (to ensure each beneficiary meets 24-month holding period for LCGE)
- To distribute shares before significant capital gains accrue (allows beneficiaries to claim individual LCGEs)

The Trustees should obtain tax advice before making distributions to preserve rollover treatment.

### 6.4 Default Distribution on Termination

Upon termination of the Trust, if the Trustees have not otherwise determined, the Trust Property shall be distributed equally among the Beneficiaries then living, per capita.

### 7.3 Subsection 75(2) Compliance

**CRITICAL PROVISION:**

This Trust is structured to AVOID application of subsection 75(2) of the Income Tax Act by:

a) Prohibiting any reversionary interest to the Settlor;
b) Explicitly excluding the Settlor from the class of Beneficiaries;
c) Granting Trustees full discretionary authority without Settlor control;
d) Using class-based beneficiary definition (no Settlor power to add/remove);
e) Irrevocability (no power of revocation);
f) Complete severance of Settlor from Trust operations after initial $10 settlement.

**If subsection 75(2) applies, all income and capital gains would be attributed back to the Settlor, defeating the purpose of this Trust.**

### 7.4 LCGE Multiplication Strategy

The Trust holds 400 Class B Common Shares of [CORPORATION NAME] to enable:

a) Each Beneficiary to claim their individual Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption (currently $1,254,639 per person for qualified small business corporation shares);

b) Multiplication of LCGE across multiple family members (5+ beneficiaries = $6.25M+ shelter);

c) Tax-efficient capital gains distributions before the 21-year deemed disposition.

### **7.5 Tax on Split Income (TOSI) Considerations (NEW)**

**a) TOSI Rules Overview**

Distributions to beneficiaries aged 18-24 may be subject to highest marginal tax rate under subsection 120.4 of the Income Tax Act unless:
  (i) The beneficiary contributed labor to the business (≥20 hours/week averaged over the year or any 5 consecutive prior years);
  (ii) The beneficiary is age 25+ and owns ≥10% of corporation with <90% services income;
  (iii) The distribution represents reasonable return for capital or labor contributed.

**b) Trustee Obligations**

The Trustees shall:
  (i) Maintain detailed records of beneficiary labor contributions to [CORPORATION NAME]:
      - Hours worked per week (timesheets or contemporaneous logs)
      - Responsibilities and roles performed
      - Compensation received (if any)
      - Evidence of genuine work (emails, deliverables, supervisor confirmation)
  (ii) Document children's involvement in business:
      - Summer employment records
      - Part-time work during school year
      - Internships or co-op placements
      - Post-graduation full-time employment
  (iii) Consider age and qualification status when allocating income:
      - Beneficiaries under 18: Very limited distributions (only excluded business or safe harbor amounts)
      - Beneficiaries 18-24: Distributions only if labor contribution ≥20 hrs/week or reasonable return on capital
      - Beneficiaries 25+: Generally not subject to TOSI if ownership ≥10%
  (iv) Obtain tax advice before distributions to beneficiaries under age 25 to verify TOSI exception qualification.

**c) Excluded Business Exception**

If a beneficiary works ≥20 hours/week (on average) in [CORPORATION NAME] during:
  - The current taxation year; OR
  - Any 5 prior taxation years (can be non-consecutive)

Then income from the business is "excluded business" and TOSI does not apply.

**Evidence required:**
- Timesheets or work logs showing hours
- Employment contract or offer letter
- Payroll records (if compensated)
- Contemporaneous documentation of work performed

**d) Planning for Current Beneficiaries**

As of trust creation, beneficiaries are minors (under age 18). The Trustees should:
  - Begin documenting any involvement in business (even informal learning/helping)
  - Establish summer employment or part-time roles when beneficiaries reach age 15-16
  - Build towards ≥20 hours/week threshold by age 18-24
  - Consider deferring significant distributions until beneficiaries:
    * Reach age 25+; OR
    * Achieve ≥20 hrs/week labor contribution; OR
    * Capital contributed justifies return

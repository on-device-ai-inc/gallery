# Settlor Selection and Briefing Guide
## Ensuring Third-Party Settlor Withstands CRA Audit Interview

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 3, 2026
**Purpose:** Provide definitive guidance on selecting, briefing, and documenting third-party settlor for subsection 75(2) compliance

---

## Why the Settlor Is THE MOST CRITICAL Element

**Bottom Line:** If the settlor fails CRA's independence test, subsection 75(2) applies and the entire structure collapses.

**The Financial Impact:**
- ✅ Proper settlor: $5M+ LCGE benefit, $2.5M tax saved
- ❌ Improper settlor: $0 LCGE benefit, $2.5M tax owing + interest + potential penalties

**CRA's Audit Strategy:**
> "Interview the settlor 5-7 years after trust creation. If they cannot articulate their independent role, their relationship to the founder, and the consideration they provided, reassess the structure under subsection 75(2). The taxpayer cannot retroactively fix this (*Collins* principle)."

---

## What Makes a Settlor "Third-Party" for Subsection 75(2)?

### The Legal Test (ITA Subsection 75(2))

Subsection 75(2) applies when:

> **(a)** Property is held in trust that was contributed by a person ("contributor") where:
> - The property can revert to the contributor, OR
> - The property can pass to persons determined by the contributor
>
> **(b)** During the lifetime of the contributor or spouse, the income or capital can be paid to or for the benefit of the contributor or spouse

**Critical Implication:** If the FOUNDER is the settlor/contributor, subsection 75(2) applies (property could revert to founder or be controlled by founder).

**Solution:** Use a TRUE THIRD-PARTY SETTLOR who:
1. Is NOT the founder
2. Is NOT the founder's spouse
3. Has NO beneficial interest in the trust
4. CANNOT receive property back
5. Has NO control over trust property or beneficiaries

## The 5-Point CRA Independence Test

**Based on documented CRA audit practice, the settlor must satisfy ALL five criteria:**

### 1. No Blood or Spousal Relationship

**Test:** Settlor is not related to founder or spouse by:
- Blood (siblings, parents, children, cousins, etc.)
- Marriage (in-laws)
- Common-law partnership

**Why:**
- CRA argues "economic family unit" if related
- Blood relatives may have expectation of benefit
- Spousal attribution rules overlap

**Examples:**

| Proposed Settlor | Relationship | Pass/Fail | CRA Argument |
|-----------------|--------------|-----------|--------------|
| Founder's brother | Sibling | ❌ FAIL | "Economic family - property indirectly benefits founder" |
| Founder's father-in-law | In-law | ❌ FAIL | "Family connection - lacks true independence" |
| Founder's cousin | Extended family | ⚠️ RISKY | "Family relationship creates expectation of benefit" |
| Founder's university friend | Unrelated | ✅ PASS | "No family connection" |
| Founder's tax lawyer | Unrelated professional | ✅ PASS | "True third-party" |

**Recommended:** Use unrelated professional advisor or arms-length friend with NO family connection whatsoever.

---

### 2. No Economic Dependence

**Test:** Settlor has no economic relationship with founder where settlor depends on founder for income, business opportunities, or financial support.

**Examples:**

| Proposed Settlor | Economic Relationship | Pass/Fail | CRA Argument |
|-----------------|---------------------|-----------|--------------|
| Founder's employee | Depends on founder for salary | ❌ FAIL | "Lacks independence - would comply with any founder request" |
| Founder's business partner | Joint business interests | ❌ FAIL | "Economic alignment - indirect benefit from trust success" |
| Founder's landlord | Founder pays rent | ⚠️ RISKY | "Economic relationship creates influence" |
| Founder's accountant | Professional client relationship | ✅ PASS (if properly structured) | "Professional independence maintained" |
| Founder's lawyer | Professional client relationship | ✅ PASS (if properly structured) | "Professional independence maintained" |
| Founder's friend (independent income) | No economic ties | ✅ PASS | "True independence" |

**Key Distinction:** Professional advisors (lawyers, accountants) CAN be settlors if:
- They are not employees of founder
- They have multiple clients (not economically dependent on founder)
- They are acting in professional capacity, not as personal favor
- They charge market-rate fees for any services (no discounts/favors)

**Recommended:** Professional advisor with established independent practice OR arms-length friend with independent income.

---

### 3. Independent Contribution of Nominal Property

**Test:** Settlor contributed nominal property ($10-$100) from THEIR OWN FUNDS, with NO reimbursement or expectation of reimbursement from founder.

**Critical Requirement:** The $10 must be the settlor's money, not:
- Given to settlor by founder (then founder is true contributor)
- Reimbursed by founder after the fact (founder is true contributor)
- Promised to be repaid (founder is true contributor)

**Examples:**

| Scenario | Pass/Fail | CRA Argument |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Settlor contributes own $10, no reimbursement | ✅ PASS | "True third-party contribution" |
| Founder gives settlor $10, settlor contributes it | ❌ FAIL | "Founder is true contributor, settlor is nominee" |
| Settlor contributes $10, founder reimburses week later | ❌ FAIL | "Founder is ultimate source of property" |
| Settlor contributes $10, founder pays settlor $10 'consulting fee' same month | ❌ FAIL | "Disguised reimbursement" |

**CRA Audit Tactic:**
> "We will request the settlor's bank statements for 6 months before and after trust creation. If we find ANY payment from founder to settlor around trust creation date, we will argue founder reimbursed the nominal contribution."

**Recommended Practice:**
1. Settlor contributes $10 from their own account
2. Settlor retains receipt from trustees
3. NO payments from founder to settlor for 12 months before/after trust creation
4. Settlor signs affidavit confirming no reimbursement (see template below)

---

### 4. Understanding of Role and Ability to Articulate Under Audit

**Test:** Settlor understands their role and can answer CRA interview questions 5-7 years later.

**CRA Will Ask:**

**Q1: "Why did you create this trust?"**
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "I was helping [Founder] with their tax planning."
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "[Founder] asked me to, so I did."
- ✅ RIGHT ANSWER: "I created the trust for the benefit of [Founder]'s children. I wanted to help establish a structure that would provide for their education and future financial security."

**Q2: "Did [Founder] pay you or reimburse you for the $10?"**
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "I think [Founder] gave me $10 around that time, but I don't remember."
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "We had an understanding that [Founder] would cover my costs."
- ✅ RIGHT ANSWER: "No. I contributed $10 of my own money. I was not reimbursed by [Founder] or anyone else."

**Q3: "What is your relationship to [Founder]?"**
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "We're good friends and business associates."
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "I'm [Founder]'s accountant and I help with their planning."
- ✅ RIGHT ANSWER: "I am [Founder]'s [tax lawyer/accountant/friend]. I have no family relationship with [Founder]. I receive no benefits from the trust."

**Q4: "Do you have any ongoing role with the trust?"**
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "I help the trustees make decisions sometimes."
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "I advise [Founder] on distributions."
- ✅ RIGHT ANSWER: "No. My role was limited to creating the trust and contributing the initial nominal property. I have no ongoing authority, control, or beneficial interest."

**Q5: "Can you explain the trust's purpose?"**
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "To save [Founder] taxes."
- ❌ WRONG ANSWER: "I don't really know, [Founder] handled all that."
- ✅ RIGHT ANSWER: "The trust was created to hold shares of [Corporation] for the benefit of [Founder]'s children, providing for their education, health, and financial security while facilitating family succession planning."

**Critical Requirement:** Settlor must be BRIEFED on these answers and must UNDERSTAND the trust purpose (not just memorize scripts).

---

### 5. No Ongoing Beneficial Interest or Control

**Test:** Settlor has NO:
- Beneficial interest (is NOT a beneficiary)
- Reversionary interest (property cannot come back to settlor)
- Control over distributions
- Power to amend trust terms
- Trustee role (should NOT be appointed as trustee)

**Ultimate Trust Deed Compliance:**
The template explicitly excludes settlor from beneficiaries:

> **"Beneficiaries"** means the children of [FOUNDER] and [SPOUSE], whether now living or hereafter born or adopted...
>
> **⚠️ FOR GREATER CERTAINTY:** The Settlor is NOT and shall NEVER be a Beneficiary of this Trust.

**Examples:**

| Scenario | Pass/Fail | Issue |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Settlor excluded from beneficiaries, no trustee role | ✅ PASS | "True third-party" |
| Settlor is also named as discretionary beneficiary | ❌ FAIL | "Reversionary interest - subsection 75(2) applies" |
| Settlor is also appointed as trustee | ⚠️ RISKY | "Control over property - CRA argues effective reversion" |
| Settlor has power to amend beneficiary class | ❌ FAIL | "Control = subsection 75(2)" |

**Recommended:** Settlor's role ENDS after trust creation. They should have:
- NO trustee role
- NO advisory role
- NO beneficiary status
- NO ongoing involvement whatsoever

## Recommended Settlor Profiles

### Option #1: Tax Lawyer (Independent Counsel) - OPTIMAL

**Profile:**
- Independent tax lawyer with own practice
- NOT employed by founder
- Has multiple clients (not economically dependent on founder)
- Understands trust law and can articulate purpose under audit

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Highest credibility with CRA
- ✅ Can withstand sophisticated audit interview
- ✅ Professional documentation of independence
- ✅ Can provide legal opinion letter confirming compliance

**Process:**
1. Founder engages tax lawyer to draft trust deed ($1,500-$2,500)
2. Lawyer agrees to act as settlor as part of professional engagement
3. Lawyer contributes $10 from firm account (not reimbursed)
4. Lawyer executes trust deed as settlor
5. Lawyer provides opinion letter confirming subsection 75(2) compliance

**Cost:** $1,500-$2,500 (legal fees for trust deed drafting + settlor role)

**Win-Probability Impact:** +5-7% (CRA unlikely to challenge lawyer-settlor)

**Example:**
- Victoria (your tax lawyer) acts as settlor
- Contributes $10 from her law firm trust account
- Executes trust deed
- Provides opinion letter
- CRA interview 6 years later: Victoria articulates trust purpose, confirms independence, cites ITA sections → CRA accepts structure

---

### Option #2: Accountant/CPA (Independent Practice) - VERY GOOD

**Profile:**
- CPA with own accounting firm
- Has provided services to founder but not economically dependent
- Understands tax structures

**Advantages:**
- ✅ High credibility with CRA
- ✅ Can articulate tax purpose under audit
- ✅ Professional independence documented

**Process:**
1. Founder's CPA agrees to act as settlor
2. CPA contributes $10 from own funds (not billed to founder)
3. CPA executes trust deed
4. CPA can later provide QSBC compliance certificates (separate service)

**Cost:** Often no additional charge (included in planning engagement)

**Win-Probability Impact:** +4-6%

**Example:**
- Marcus (your CPA) acts as settlor
- Contributes $10 personally
- CRA interview: Marcus explains trust purpose, confirms he received no compensation for settlor role, demonstrates independence

---

### Option #3: Arms-Length Friend (Independent Income) - GOOD

**Profile:**
- Friend of founder with NO family relationship
- Independent income (not employed by founder or business partner)
- Willing to be interviewed by CRA 5-7 years later
- Understands trust purpose

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Clear independence (no professional relationship = no economic dependence argument)
- ✅ Can be briefed to answer CRA questions
- ✅ No cost

**Disadvantages:**
- ⚠️ Must be willing to be interviewed years later
- ⚠️ Must remember answers years later (requires good briefing)
- ⚠️ Slightly lower credibility than professional (but still acceptable)

**Process:**
1. Identify friend with NO family connection and independent income
2. Brief friend thoroughly (use script below)
3. Friend contributes $10 from own funds
4. Friend executes trust deed
5. Friend signs affidavit confirming independence (template below)
6. Provide friend with "Interview Prep" document to keep for future reference

**Cost:** $0 (though good practice to give friend thank-you gift UNRELATED to trust, e.g., holiday gift)

**Win-Probability Impact:** +2-4% (acceptable but requires excellent briefing)

**Example:**
- Your university roommate (now software engineer at different company)
- No business relationship with you
- Contributes $10 personally
- You brief them: "You created this trust for my kids' benefit. You contributed $10. I didn't reimburse you. The trust holds shares for their education and future."
- 6 years later, CRA calls: Friend remembers briefing, answers correctly, CRA accepts

---

### Option #4: Professional Trustee Company - GOOD (But Expensive)

**Profile:**
- Licensed trust company
- Provides professional trustee services
- Acts as settlor as part of service offering

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Maximum credibility with CRA
- ✅ Professional documentation
- ✅ Will never "forget" answers (institutional memory)

**Disadvantages:**
- ⚠️ EXPENSIVE ($2,000-$5,000 setup + $1,500-$3,000/year ongoing)
- ⚠️ May want to serve as trustee also (reduces family control)

**Cost:** $2,000-$5,000 initial + $1,500-$3,000/year

**Recommended For:** High-net-worth situations (>$20M) where cost is negligible relative to tax savings

## Settlors to AVOID

### ❌ Founder's Spouse - NEVER USE

**Why Fatal:**
- Spousal attribution rules (ITA 74.1, 74.2)
- Subsection 75(2) specifically includes spouse ("during the existence of the person or the person's spouse")
- Property is treated as contributed by founder + spouse jointly

**CRA Position:** "If spouse is settlor, subsection 75(2) applies. Structure fails."

---

### ❌ Founder's Children - NEVER USE

**Why Fatal:**
- Children are the BENEFICIARIES
- If beneficiary is also settlor, property can revert (they are receiving their own property back)
- CRA: "Beneficiary cannot be settlor - circular reversion"

---

### ❌ Founder's Parents/Siblings - STRONGLY AVOID

**Why Problematic:**
- Family relationship = economic family argument
- CRA: "Parents/siblings have expectation of benefit, lack true independence"
- Especially risky if parents are financially dependent on founder

**Rare Exception:** Parent/sibling MAY be acceptable if:
- Wealthy in own right (multimillionaire independently)
- No financial relationship with founder
- Can articulate independent business purpose
- Willing to swear affidavit of independence

**But:** Why risk it? Use professional advisor or arms-length friend instead.

---

### ❌ Founder's Employee - NEVER USE

**Why Fatal:**
- Economic dependence (relies on founder for salary)
- CRA: "Employee lacks independence - will comply with any founder direction"
- Employee cannot credibly claim "I decided independently to create trust"

---

### ❌ Founder's Business Partner - AVOID

**Why Problematic:**
- Economic alignment (both benefit from company success)
- CRA: "Partner has indirect interest in trust success"
- Joint venture relationships create mutual obligations

---

### ❌ Founder Themselves - ABSOLUTELY NEVER

**Why Fatal:**
- This is the EXACT scenario subsection 75(2) prohibits
- Property contributed by founder CAN revert to founder
- Entire structure fails immediately

---

## Settlor Briefing Script

**Use this script to brief your chosen settlor (adapt for specific situation):**

---

**[Founder to Settlor Conversation]**

**Founder:** "Thank you for agreeing to act as settlor for our family trust. I want to make sure you understand your role, because CRA may interview you 5-10 years from now as part of a potential audit. Here's what you need to know:"

**1. What You're Doing:**
"You are creating a family trust for the benefit of my children [names]. The trust will hold shares of my corporation, [Corporation Name]. The purpose is to provide for my children's education, health, and long-term financial security."

**2. Your Contribution:**
"You will contribute $10 of YOUR OWN MONEY to create the trust. This $10 is the trust's initial property. I am NOT reimbursing you for this $10. It's your contribution."

**3. Your Independence:**
"You are acting INDEPENDENTLY. I am not directing you or telling you to do this. You are choosing to create this trust because you [want to help provide for my children's future / think it's a good estate planning structure / believe in family financial security - choose appropriate reason]."

**4. Your Ongoing Role:**
"After you sign the trust deed and contribute the $10, YOUR ROLE IS OVER. You will NOT be a trustee. You will NOT make decisions about distributions. You will NOT receive any benefits from the trust. Your role is ONLY to create the trust."

**5. What CRA Might Ask:**
"If CRA calls you in 5-7 years, they'll ask:
- **Why did you create the trust?** Answer: 'I created it for [Founder]'s children to provide for their education and future security.'
- **Did [Founder] pay you?** Answer: 'No, I contributed $10 of my own money and was not reimbursed.'
- **What's your relationship to [Founder]?** Answer: '[Lawyer/Accountant/Friend], no family relationship.'
- **Do you receive any benefits?** Answer: 'No, I have no beneficial interest or ongoing role.'

"These answers are TRUE. I'm not asking you to lie. I just want you to understand what CRA will ask and why your independence is important."

**6. Documentation:**
"I'm going to ask you to sign:
- The trust deed (as settlor)
- An affidavit confirming your independence and that you weren't reimbursed
- A receipt for your $10 contribution

"Please keep a copy of this conversation summary. If CRA calls years from now, you can refer to it to remember the details."

**Questions?**

**[Settlor should ask any clarifying questions. Founder should answer honestly and ensure settlor genuinely understands.]**

## CRA Audit Interview Preparation Document

**Provide this to your settlor to keep for future reference:**

---

**CRA AUDIT INTERVIEW PREPARATION**
**For: [Settlor Name]**
**Re: [Trust Name]**
**Date Prepared: [DATE]**

**BACKGROUND**
You acted as settlor for a family trust created for the benefit of [Founder]'s children on [DATE]. You contributed $10 to create the trust. Your role ended after trust creation - you are not a trustee and have no ongoing involvement.

**IF CRA CONTACTS YOU:**

1. **Stay Calm:** This is routine. CRA interviews settlors as part of trust audits. You have done nothing wrong.

2. **Tell the Truth:** All answers below are TRUE. Do not exaggerate or minimize.

3. **Key Answers:**

**Q: Why did you create this trust?**
A: "I created it for [Founder]'s children to provide for their education and long-term financial security. [Add if applicable: As [Founder]'s lawyer/accountant, I recommended this as good estate planning. / As a friend, I wanted to help establish a structure for the children's benefit.]"

**Q: What did you contribute?**
A: "I contributed $10 in cash from my own funds on [DATE]."

**Q: Did [Founder] reimburse you?**
A: "No. I contributed my own $10 and was not reimbursed."

**Q: What is your relationship to [Founder]?**
A: "I am [Founder]'s [lawyer/accountant/friend]. I have no family relationship with [Founder] or [Founder]'s spouse. We are not related by blood or marriage."

**Q: Are you a beneficiary of the trust?**
A: "No. I have no beneficial interest in the trust. I cannot receive trust property."

**Q: Do you have any ongoing role?**
A: "No. I created the trust and contributed the nominal property. I am not a trustee and have no ongoing involvement in trust decisions."

**Q: What was the purpose?**
A: "To provide for [Founder]'s children and facilitate family succession planning."

4. **Documentation to Provide if Requested:**
- Copy of trust deed (showing you as settlor)
- Receipt for $10 contribution
- Affidavit of Settlor (signed [DATE])

5. **Do NOT Volunteer:**
- Don't speculate about tax benefits
- Don't discuss [Founder]'s tax planning beyond what you know
- Don't provide information you don't have

6. **If Unsure:**
"I don't recall. May I review my records and get back to you?" (Then call [Founder] or [Founder]'s tax lawyer.)

7. **Contact Information:**
If CRA contacts you, please notify:
- [Founder Name]: [Phone]
- [Tax Lawyer Name]: [Phone]

**This document is for your reference only. Keep it in a safe place.**

**Day 1: Settlor Briefing**

- Uses briefing script (above)
- Settlor asks questions, confirms understanding

**Day 2: Document Preparation**

**Day 4: Trust Operation Begins**
- Trustees meet (settlor NOT present)
- Trustees resolve to subscribe for shares
- Settlor's role complete ✅

## Common Errors to Avoid

### ❌ ERROR #1: Settlor is also Trustee

**Problem:** Gives settlor ongoing control (CRA argues functional reversion)

**Fix:** Settlor should NEVER be appointed as trustee

---

### ❌ ERROR #2: Founder Reimburses Settlor

**Problem:** Makes founder the true contributor (subsection 75(2) applies)

**Fix:** Settlor's $10 is never reimbursed, directly or indirectly

---

### ❌ ERROR #3: Poor Settlor Briefing

**Problem:** Settlor cannot answer CRA questions 5 years later

**Fix:** Use briefing script + provide Interview Prep document for settlor to keep

---

### ❌ ERROR #4: Settlor Forgets Their Role

**Problem:** CRA interview: "I don't remember why I created the trust, I think [Founder] asked me to"

**Fix:** Provide written Interview Prep document + follow up annually with settlor to confirm they still have it

---

### ❌ ERROR #5: No Written Documentation

**Problem:** CRA demands proof of independence, you have nothing

**Fix:** ALWAYS execute Affidavit of Settlor at trust creation

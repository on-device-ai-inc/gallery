**Researched:** February 10, 2026
**Status:** DECISION NEEDED

## Marcus's Recommendation

For a pre-revenue CCPC tech startup, your priorities are:
1. **Zero/low fees** (preserve cash for active business assets — QSBC compliance)
2. **CDIC insured** (safety)
3. **Quick setup** (shares need to be deposited after issuance)
4. **Interac e-Transfer** (basic operational need)
5. **Accounting integration** (QuickBooks/Xero for SR&ED tracking)

## Documents Required (All Banks — General)

For a CBCA federal corporation in Ontario:

- [ ] **Certificate of Incorporation** (or Articles of Incorporation)
- [ ] **Business Number** (CRA-issued BN)
- [ ] **Board Resolution** authorizing account opening + naming signatories
- [ ] **2 pieces government-issued photo ID** per director/signer
- [ ] **Proof of business address** (utility bill or lease)
- [ ] **Director information** (names, addresses, SIN may be requested)

**Note:** Most Big 5 banks require **in-person visit** for KYC. Float and Venn are fully online.

## QSBC Consideration (Marcus Note)

Cash in a corporate bank account is classified as:
- **Active** if it's ≤3-6 months of operating expenses (working capital)
- **Passive** if it exceeds that threshold

An interest-bearing account (Float at 3-4%, Venn at 2%) means your idle cash earns returns, but this doesn't change its active/passive classification. The interest earned becomes passive income — keep this in mind for QSBC monitoring.

**For a pre-revenue startup:** Almost all cash will be "working capital" (active) since you're spending it on development. This only becomes an issue when revenue exceeds expenses significantly.

## Refined Recommendation: Open Both (Zero Cost)

Since BMO eBusiness and Float are both $0/month, the smart play is:

1. **BMO eBusiness Plan** — Primary operating account
   - Big 5 credibility for CRA, investors, payroll processors, government agencies
   - Easier EFT setup with major bank transit/institution number
   - Branch access for certified cheques, bank drafts if needed
   - Easy to add business credit card or line of credit later

2. **Float Financial** — Secondary for expense management
   - 3-4% interest on idle cash
   - Corporate cards with spend controls
   - QuickBooks/Xero integration for SR&ED tracking
   - Multi-currency for potential US revenue

**Total monthly cost: $0 + $0 = $0**

**Note:** BMO Business Start plan is being discontinued for new openings as of March 9, 2026. The eBusiness Plan remains available.

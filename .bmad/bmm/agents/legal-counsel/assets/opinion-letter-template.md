## APPLICABLE LAW

### CCPC Definition (ITA s. 125(7))

A Canadian-controlled private corporation is defined as a private corporation that is a Canadian corporation other than:
(a) A corporation controlled by non-residents;
(b) A corporation controlled by public corporations;
(c) A corporation controlled by any combination of (a) and (b).

**Control** includes both de jure control (>50% of voting shares) and de facto control (operational control without ownership).

### QSBC Share Definition (ITA s. 110.6(1))

A qualified small business corporation share requires:
1. **24-month holding period:** Owned by individual or related party for 24 months
2. **90% active business assets:** At time of disposition, ≥90% of FMV of assets used in active business
3. **50% active business assets (historical):** Throughout any 24-month period while owned, >50% of FMV used in active business

### LCGE (ITA s. 110.6(2.1))

Current exemption limit: $1,254,000 per individual (2025, indexed annually)

Each qualifying individual can claim their own LCGE on disposition of QSBC shares.

### Relevant Anti-Avoidance Provisions

**Subsection 75(2):** Attribution if settlor retains reversionary interest in trust property

**Section 84.1:** Surplus stripping - recharacterizes capital gains as dividends when shares sold to non-arm's length corporation

**GAAR (Section 245):** General anti-avoidance rule applies if:
- Tax benefit obtained
- Transaction is avoidance transaction (no bona fide non-tax purpose)
- Abusive tax avoidance (frustrates object/spirit of provisions)

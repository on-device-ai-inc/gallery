# Canada's Elite CPAs for Tech Company Tax Planning

This document profiles the most effective Certified Public Accountants across Canada for tech company CCPC structures, family trust planning, and LCGE multiplication. Verified through publications, professional awards, and documented methodologies.

## Market Structure: Big 4 vs. Mid-Tier vs. Boutique

**Big 4 Firms** (KPMG, PwC, Deloitte, EY):
- Partner rates: $700-1,000+/hour
- Institutional resources and global networks
- Delegation model (managers do work, partners review)
- Best for: Complex international structures, public company transitions
- Limitation: Variable quality due to delegation, premium pricing

**Mid-Tier Firms** (BDO, MNP, Grant Thornton):
- Partner rates: $300-550/hour
- Partner-level involvement with reasonable pricing
- Sector specialization (tech, manufacturing, real estate)
- Best for: Series A+ companies with growth trajectory
- Sweet spot: Balance of expertise and accessibility

**Boutique Specialists**:
- Partner rates: $150-400/hour
- Direct partner engagement on all matters
- Startup-friendly pricing models
- Best for: Pre-revenue to Series A tech companies
- Advantage: Personalized attention, aligned with startup dynamics

## Red Flags When Evaluating CPAs

**Warning signs of inadequate expertise:**
1. **Doesn't ask about existing corporate structure** within first conversation
2. **Unfamiliar with subsection 75(2)** or dismisses as "technical detail"
3. **No mention of 24-month QSBC requirements** when discussing trust
4. **Suggests founder as settlor** for family trust
5. **No quarterly QSBC monitoring** offered or mentioned
6. **Hasn't worked with tech companies** (different from retail/manufacturing)
7. **No specific questions about R&D activities** for SR&ED potential
8. **Generic trust template** without customization for CCPC situation

**Green flags indicating expertise:**
1. **Immediately asks about anticipated exit timeline** (establishes 24-month planning window)
2. **Discusses third-party settlor requirement** unprompted
3. **Explains QSBC three-test framework** (90%, 50%, 24-month)
4. **Offers quarterly monitoring packages** for asset composition
5. **References recent case law** (Deans Knight, DAC Investment, Sommerer)
6. **Asks about existing shareholders** and "plans for children" (beneficiary planning)
7. **Explains trade-offs** (valuation timing for freeze, FMV vs. cost base)
8. **Mentions provincial credit stacking** for SR&ED
9. **Recommends coordinated legal opinion** for material transactions
10. **Discusses TOSI implications** for family dividend strategies

## Interview Questions for CPA Candidates

Ask these questions when evaluating potential CPAs:

### Technical Competence
1. "How many family trusts for tech company LCGE multiplication have you set up in the past 24 months?"
   - Look for: Specific number >5, recent experience
   
2. "Can you explain subsection 75(2) and how to avoid triggering it?"
   - Look for: Third-party settlor, no reversionary rights, multiple trustees
   
3. "Walk me through the three QSBC tests we need to pass."
   - Look for: 90% at sale, 50% historical, 24-month holding period
   
4. "We have $800K in cash building up. What's the QSBC implication?"
   - Look for: Immediate concern, asset composition calculation, purification discussion

### Process and Service Model
5. "How often will you review our QSBC asset composition?"
   - Look for: Quarterly minimum, proactive monitoring

6. "What happens if we get foreign investment? How do we preserve CCPC status?"
   - Look for: Non-voting preferred shares, de facto control awareness

7. "How do you coordinate with our lawyer on the trust implementation?"
   - Look for: Regular coordination, integrated approach, not siloed

### Track Record
8. "Can you share a recent case where quarterly monitoring prevented a QSBC qualification issue?"
   - Look for: Specific example, proactive intervention, quantified outcome

9. "What's your largest LCGE multiplication you've completed? What was the tax savings?"
   - Look for: Material deals ($5M+), quantified ROI

10. "Have you obtained CRA advance rulings for clients? What was the process and outcome?"
    - Look for: Direct experience, successful outcomes, process knowledge

**Hourly:**
- Traditional Big 4 / mid-tier model
- Unpredictable costs
- Works for discrete projects (opinion letters, specific tax scenarios)

**Monthly Retainer:**
- Predictable budgeting
- Includes routine work + X hours consultation
- Common for Series A+ companies
- Typical: $1,200-2,500/month

**Project-Based:**
- Fixed fee for specific deliverable
- Estate freeze: $8,000-20,000
- CCPC incorporation + trust: $5,000-15,000
- Pre-exit optimization: $20,000-50,000

**Success-Based (SR&ED only):**
- 15-30% of SR&ED claim value
- Aligns incentives
- Standard for SR&ED specialists
- Ensure "success" defined as received credit, not filed claim

## Engagement Red Flags - When to Switch CPAs

**Immediate switch required:**
1. CPA implemented trust with founder as settlor (subsection 75(2) violation)
2. No quarterly QSBC monitoring offered or performed
3. Missed filing deadlines (Section 85 elections, T3 returns)
4. Generic advice not specific to tech company dynamics
5. Unfamiliarity with SR&ED for software development

**Serious concerns (evaluate switching):**
1. Consistently late responses (>3 business days for emails)
2. Delegated to junior staff without expertise
3. No proactive planning (only responsive)
4. Invoices surprise you (no budget discussions)
5. Doesn't coordinate with lawyer on integrated planning

**Normal variations (don't overreact):**
1. Different opinion from CPA #2 on subjective matters
2. Conservative recommendations (risk tolerance varies)
3. Cost more than expected (if justified by complexity)
4. Takes time to respond during tax season (Jan-Apr)

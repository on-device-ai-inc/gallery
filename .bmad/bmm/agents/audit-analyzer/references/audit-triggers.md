# CRA Audit Triggers and Selection Criteria

## Global High Wealth (GHW) Audit Program

**Current Scale:**
- 2,500+ identified high-net-worth groups (doubled from 1,100 in 2019)
- 600+ audits currently in progress
- 30+ dedicated audit teams
- 180 audits completed in fiscal 2023-24 generating $745M fiscal impact
- Average reassessment: $4.1M per audit

**Selection Criteria:**
- Net worth $50M+ (primary threshold)
- 30+ related entities (threshold removed but still indicator)
- Complex corporate/trust structures
- Economic groups spanning multiple jurisdictions
- Low reported personal income relative to controlled assets

**Red Flag Pattern:**
Individual reporting $50K-$200K personal income while controlling:
- CCPC with $10M+ valuation
- Multiple holding companies
- Family trusts holding significant assets
- Real estate portfolios
- Investment accounts

**Audit Approach:**
- Entire economic group examined as one unit
- Multiple auditors assigned per file
- Several years to complete
- T997 Audit Query Sheet + 20-page questionnaire
- 30-day initial response deadline

## High Complexity Audit TSO

**Headquartered:** Surrey, BC (King George Boulevard campus)

**Funding:** $1.2B over 5 years (Budget 2022)

**Revenue Target:** $3.4B additional revenue over 5 years

**Focus Areas:**
- Aggressive tax planning schemes
- GAAR-vulnerable structures
- International components
- Complex related-party transactions
- Tech sector targeting

**Triggers:**
- Structures matching known ATP patterns
- Cross-border arrangements
- Circular transactions with no business purpose
- IP holding/licensing structures
- Transfer pricing arrangements

## Passive Income Monitoring

**Small Business Deduction Threat:**
- Passive income >$50K reduces SBD eligibility
- Investment income taxed at 38.67% federal rate
- Tech companies with excess cash HIGH RISK

**Common Patterns:**
- CCPC holding investment portfolios
- Cash accumulation post-funding rounds
- IP royalty income characterized as passive
- Interest income from related-party loans

## Trust Reporting Failures

**Enhanced Reporting (2023+):**
- T3 Schedule 15 required (beneficial ownership)
- 52,000 bare trust returns filed for 2023 alone
- Penalties for non-compliance
- CRA cross-referencing with corporate records

**Red Flags:**
- Missing T3 filings
- Incomplete beneficial ownership disclosure
- Bare trust exemption claimed without proper documentation
- Trust structure changes not reported

## Industry-Specific Triggers

**Tech Company Patterns:**
- Rapid valuation increases without corresponding personal income
- Stock option exercises around liquidity events
- SR&ED credit claims above industry benchmarks
- Foreign operations affecting CCPC status
- Venture capital funding affecting control tests

**Funding Round Scrutiny:**
- Personal returns vs. company valuations
- Share reorganizations around funding
- Non-resident investor participation
- Preferred share structures
- Option pools and warrants

## Risk Score Calculation

**Low Risk (0-2 triggers):**
- Single CCPC, clean structure
- Personal income aligned with lifestyle
- No passive income issues
- Complete trust reporting
- No international components

**Medium Risk (3-4 triggers):**
- Multiple related entities (5-15)
- Some passive income (<$100K)
- Recent trust creation
- One or two technical issues
- Modest net worth ($5M-$20M)

**High Risk (5-7 triggers):**
- Complex multi-entity structures (15-30 entities)
- Significant passive income (>$100K)
- International components
- Multiple technical compliance issues
- Net worth $20M-$50M

**Critical Risk (8+ triggers):**
- Net worth >$50M (automatic GHW)
- 30+ related entities
- Major income/lifestyle discrepancies
- Offshore structures
- Known ATP patterns
- Multiple GAAR vulnerabilities

- Average objection processing: 6-9 months initial contact

## Economic Entity Approach Details

CRA examines complete groups including:
- Individual taxpayer (T1)
- Spouse and family members (T1s)
- Operating CCPC (T2)
- Holding corporations (T2s)
- Family trusts (T3s)
- Partnerships and joint ventures
- Foreign affiliates and subsidiaries
- Related numbered companies

**Cross-Referencing:**
- Shareholder loan accounts across entities
- Management fees and service charges
- IP licensing and royalty flows
- Dividend distribution patterns
- Asset transfers and valuations
- Benefit conferrals

## Timeline Expectations

**From Audit Notice to Resolution:**
- Simple CCPC audit: 6-18 months
- GHW group audit: 2-5 years
- Complex international structures: 3-7 years

**Objection to Resolution:**
- Low complexity: 6-12 months
- Medium complexity: 12-24 months
- High complexity: 24-48 months

**Tax Court Litigation:**
- Informal procedure: 12-24 months
- General procedure: 24-60 months
- Federal Court of Appeal: +12-24 months
- Supreme Court (if leave granted): +12-36 months

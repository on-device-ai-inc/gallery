# Case Law Database: CCPC + Family Trust Precedents

## Major Taxpayer Victories

### Sommerer v. Canada (2012 FCA 207)

**Issue:** Subsection 75(2) attribution when beneficiary sells property to trust at FMV

**Holding:** TAXPAYER WIN - Subsection 75(2) applies only to settlors and subsequent contributors, not beneficiaries who sell property at FMV

**Key Principle:** Fair market value sales to trusts do not trigger attribution

**Application:** Beneficiaries can sell QSBC shares to family trusts at FMV without 75(2) attribution, preserving LCGE eligibility

**Confidence Boost:** +20% for structures involving FMV sales to trusts

### Alta Energy Luxembourg (2021 SCC 49)

**Issue:** GAAR application to treaty shopping arrangement

**Holding:** TAXPAYER WIN (6-3) - GAAR cannot rewrite negotiated treaty provisions

**Amount at Stake:** $380M capital gain exemption preserved

**Key Principle:** "Tax treaties are contracts between sovereign nations; Canada cannot unilaterally 'revisit its bargain'"

**Application:** Parliament's deliberate regime choices and treaty bargains are protected from GAAR

**Confidence Boost:** +15-20% for structures based on legitimate regime selection

### DAC Investment Holdings (2024 TCC 63)

**Issue:** CCPC continuing out of Canada to avoid refundable tax

**Holding:** TAXPAYER WIN - Moving between tax regimes with different attributes is not inherently abusive

**Amount at Stake:** $2.36M gain, avoiding refundable tax

**Key Quote:** "Moving from one taxing regime with its pluses and minuses to another taxing regime with different pluses and minuses" is not abusive

**Status:** Under appeal by CRA; closed by Bill C-59 substantive CCPC rules (post-April 7, 2022)

**Application:** Parliament's creation of different regimes for different corporation types reflects deliberate policy choice

**Confidence Boost:** +10-15% for structures based on statutory regime differences

### Pellerin v. Canada (2015 TCC 130)

**Issue:** Whether infant beneficiary satisfied 24-month QSBC holding period

**Holding:** TAXPAYER WIN - Definition requires only that shares not be owned by unrelated persons

**Application:** Family trust holdings can satisfy 24-month requirement for LCGE purposes

**Confidence Boost:** +10% for trust-based LCGE planning

### Brent Kern Family Trust (2013 TCC 54)

**Issue:** Taxpayer attempting to trigger 75(2) attribution for tax advantage

**Holding:** TAXPAYER WIN - Proper application of Sommerer principles

**Key Finding:** Each property transfer analyzed separately for 75(2) application

**Application:** Property-by-property analysis allows mixed structures

**Confidence Boost:** +5-10% for properly documented trust acquisitions

## Major CRA Victories

### Deans Knight Income Corp (2023 SCC 16)

**Issue:** GAAR application to loss restriction rule avoidance

**Holding:** CRA WIN (7-1) - "Functional equivalence" test established

**Key Principle:** Achieving functional equivalent of prohibited outcome triggers GAAR regardless of technical compliance

**Critical Quote:** Company became "an empty vessel" for unused deductions; "gutted of any vestiges from its prior corporate 'life'"

**Post-Decision Impact:**
- Bill C-59 added 25% GAAR penalty
- Expanded reassessment periods by 3 years
- "One of the main purposes" test (lower threshold)

**Application:** Structures that defeat purpose of provisions face GAAR even if technically compliant

**Confidence Penalty:** -20-30% for structures achieving functional equivalent of prohibited outcomes

### Collins Family Trust (2022 SCC 26)

**Issue:** Equitable rescission after CRA interpretation change

**Holding:** CRA WIN (8-1) - No "undo button" for tax planning mistakes

**Key Principle:** "Taxpayers should be taxed based on what they actually agreed to do and did, and not on what they could have done or later wished they had done"

**Application:** Structures must be correct from inception; CRA interpretation changes cannot be remedied retroactively

**Critical Implication:** Cannot fix 75(2) attribution or QSBC failures after the fact

**Confidence Penalty:** -25% for structures with technical errors that cannot be remedied

### Garron Family Trust (2012 SCC 43)

**Issue:** Residence of offshore trust for tax purposes

**Holding:** CRA WIN - Central management and control test determines trust residence

**Amount at Stake:** $152M in withholding taxes upheld

**Key Principle:** Physical location of trustee insufficient; actual decision-making control determines residence

**Application:** Canadian beneficiaries controlling trust decisions = Canadian-resident trust

**Confidence Penalty:** -15-20% for offshore trust structures with Canadian control

### D'Arcy (2025 TCC - example of recent CRA win)

**Issue:** PUC averaging to circumvent section 84.1

**Holding:** CRA WIN - Surplus stripping through PUC averaging is abusive

**Application:** Post-*Deans Knight* courts applying functional equivalence broadly

**Confidence Penalty:** -10-15% for structures involving PUC manipulation

### Durocher (2016 FCA 299)

**Issue:** Call options affecting CCPC status and QSBC qualification

**Holding:** CRA WIN - Options granted to third parties deemed control, destroying CCPC status

**Application:** Shareholders' agreements must be reviewed for options/warrants that could retroactively destroy QSBC qualification

**Confidence Penalty:** -20% if any third-party options or warrants exist

## Mixed/Conditional Outcomes

### Poulin / Turgeon (Tax Court)

**Facts:** Both involved arm's length sales to claim LCGE

**Poulin:** TAXPAYER WIN - Genuine exit with separate commercial interests

**Turgeon:** CRA WIN - Purchasing company was "accommodation party" with no commercial benefit

**Key Distinction:** Genuine vs. artificial commercial purpose

**Application:** Arm's length crystallizations require real commercial substance

**Confidence Impact:** Depends on genuine business purpose evidence

## GAAR Litigation Success Rates

**Historical (pre-*Deans Knight*):**
- 54 GAAR cases litigated through 2014
- 28 CRA wins (52% success rate)
- 26 taxpayer wins (48% success rate)

**Post-*Deans Knight* (2023-2025):**
- CRA success rate increasing
- Functional equivalence doctrine applied broadly
- Courts more receptive to CRA GAAR arguments

## Objection Success Rates

**Overall Statistics:**
- 60% of objections fully or partially allowed
- Means CRA auditors frequently overreach in initial assessments
- Higher success rate for well-documented structures
- Lower success rate for novel aggressive planning

**By Dispute Type:**
- Technical compliance issues: 70-75% taxpayer success
- GAAR challenges: 40-50% taxpayer success (declining post-*Deans Knight*)
- Valuation disputes: 55-65% settlement/split decisions
- Net worth audits: 45-60% taxpayer success with good records

## Applying Case Law to New Structures

**Favorable Alignment Indicators:**
- FMV transactions (Sommerer)
- Legitimate regime selection (Alta Energy, DAC Investment)
- Genuine non-tax purposes (Alta Energy, Poulin)
- Clear statutory authorization (Pellerin)

**Unfavorable Alignment Indicators:**
- Circular transactions with no business purpose (Deans Knight)
- Achieving functional equivalent of prohibited outcome (Deans Knight)
- Offshore control from Canada (Garron)
- Accommodation parties with no real interest (Turgeon)
- Technical errors that cannot be fixed (Collins)

## Recent Trends (2023-2025)

**Increasingly Favorable to CRA:**
- Functional equivalence doctrine from *Deans Knight*
- Bill C-59 GAAR amendments (25% penalty, extended reassessment)
- Courts emphasizing substance over form
- Greater skepticism of technical compliance arguments

**Still Favorable to Taxpayers:**
- Regime selection remains protected (*Alta Energy*)
- FMV transactions still avoid attribution (*Sommerer* still good law)
- Genuine business purposes still protected
- Technical compliance still matters when combined with substance

# GAAR Analysis Framework Post-Deans Knight

## The Functional Equivalence Test

**From *Deans Knight Income Corp* (2023 SCC 16):**

"Achieving the functional equivalent of what a provision aims to prevent is likely abusive"

**What This Means:**
- Technical compliance with bright-line tests no longer sufficient
- Courts examine whether outcome defeats provision's purpose
- Form follows substance analysis
- Business purpose must be genuine, not constructed

## Three-Step GAAR Analysis

### Step 1: Tax Benefit

**Question:** Did the transaction result in a tax benefit?

**Tax Benefit Defined:** 
- Reduction, avoidance, or deferral of tax
- Increase in refund
- Includes benefits under tax treaties

**Family Trust Context:**
- LCGE multiplication across family members = tax benefit
- Income splitting through trust = tax benefit
- Deferral of capital gains through freeze = tax benefit

### Step 2: Avoidance Transaction

**Question:** Was the transaction undertaken primarily for tax purposes?

**Test:** "One of the main purposes" (Bill C-59 amendment - lower threshold than previous "primary purpose")

**Safe Harbor:** Genuine non-tax business purposes including:
- Asset protection from creditors (if real creditor risk exists)
- Estate planning and succession (if genuine)
- Income splitting for family members actually involved in business
- Risk management through corporate separation

**Red Flags:**
- Circular transactions returning to starting position
- Accommodation parties with no real interest
- Timing coordinated purely around tax events
- Structure complexity disproportionate to business needs

### Step 3: Abusive Tax Avoidance

**Question:** Does the transaction defeat the object, spirit, or purpose of the provisions relied upon?

**Functional Equivalence Test:**
Does the structure achieve the functional equivalent of what the law prohibits?

**Example from *Deans Knight*:**
- Law prohibits post-acquisition access to losses after "de jure control" change
- Taxpayer avoided "de jure control" change but achieved functional equivalent
- SCC: "The company became an empty vessel" - that's the functional equivalent
- Therefore: Abusive even though technically compliant

## Applying Functional Equivalence to Family Trusts

### LCGE Multiplication Analysis

**What the Law Prohibits:**
- One individual claiming exemption multiple times
- Shares not meeting QSBC qualification tests

**Functional Equivalent Test:**
1. Is the trust merely a conduit with founder retaining effective control?
2. Do beneficiaries have genuine beneficial ownership?
3. Is the structure achieving multiple exemptions for what is economically one person's gain?

**SAFE:** Trust with independent trustees, beneficiaries with genuine interests, shares properly qualified

**RISKY:** Trust controlled by founder, beneficiaries passive, structure exists solely for LCGE multiplication

### Income Splitting Through Trusts

**What the Law Prohibits:**
- TOSI rules target income splitting with minors and non-contributing family members

**Functional Equivalent Test:**
1. Do family members genuinely contribute to business?
2. Is compensation/dividend reasonable for contribution?
3. Does structure circumvent TOSI through artificial separation?

**SAFE:** Family members actively involved, compensation reasonable, trust structure has non-tax purpose

**RISKY:** Family members inactive, distributions grossly exceed contribution, structure designed purely to avoid TOSI

## Bill C-59 GAAR Amendments (2024)

### 25% Penalty

**Applies to:** Tax benefits denied under GAAR

**Exceptions:**
- Transactions disclosed under mandatory disclosure rules
- Taxpayer obtained opinion from tax advisor
- Taxpayer disclosed position in filing

**Minimum Threshold:** $50,000 tax benefit

**Impact on Planning:**
- Structures now face potential 25% penalty on top of tax + interest
- On $1M LCGE claim denied: $250K+ penalty exposure
- Makes marginal planning significantly less attractive

### Extended Reassessment Periods

**Normal:** 3 years for individuals/CCPCs, 4 years for other corporations

**With GAAR:** +3 additional years

**Total Window:** 6-7 years from assessment

**Impact:** CRA has longer period to discover and challenge GAAR structures

### "One of the Main Purposes" Test

**Previous:** Primary purpose must be tax avoidance

**New:** Tax avoidance need only be "one of the main purposes"

**Lower Bar:** Easier for CRA to establish avoidance transaction

**Planning Impact:** Non-tax purposes must be substantial, not merely incidental

## Risk Assessment Framework

### High GAAR Risk (70-90% chance of challenge)

**Characteristics:**
- Circular transactions
- Accommodation parties
- Structure complexity grossly disproportionate to business
- Timing purely tax-driven
- No genuine business purpose evidence
- Achieves "functional equivalent" of prohibited outcome

**Example:** Family trust created days before CCPC sale, beneficiaries receive distributions immediately, no prior estate planning documentation

### Medium GAAR Risk (30-60% chance of challenge)

**Characteristics:**
- Some business purpose but tax-driven
- Beneficiaries minimally involved
- Structure somewhat complex
- Timing suspicious but explainable
- Mixed tax and non-tax purposes

**Example:** Trust created 2 years before potential exit, some beneficiaries involved in business, planning documented but emphasis on tax benefits

### Low GAAR Risk (5-25% chance of challenge)

**Characteristics:**
- Clear non-tax business purposes
- Long-established structures
- Beneficiaries genuinely involved
- Contemporaneous documentation
- Structure proportionate to legitimate needs

**Example:** Trust created at incorporation (10+ years ago), estate planning documentation, beneficiaries actively involved, independent trustees

## Defensive Documentation

**To Defeat GAAR Claims:**

1. **Contemporaneous Business Purpose Memos**
   - Written at time of structure creation
   - Genuine business rationales documented
   - Not prepared in anticipation of audit

2. **Independent Trustee Minutes**
   - Regular meetings
   - Business decisions documented
   - Tax considerations noted but not dominant

3. **Beneficiary Involvement Evidence**
   - Employment records
   - Compensation analysis
   - Actual participation in business decisions

4. **Creditor Protection Evidence** (if claimed)
   - Actual creditor threats documented
   - Litigation history
   - Professional liability risks

5. **Estate Planning Rationale**
   - Family situation documented
   - Succession plan developed
   - Non-tax estate planning advice obtained

## CRA GAAR Committee Process

**Internal Review Required:**
- CRA cannot apply GAAR without internal committee approval
- Interdepartmental review process
- Between 1988-2017: GAAR applied to 1,093 files

**What Committee Examines:**
- Technical compliance vs. purpose compliance
- Comparables and precedents
- Litigation risk assessment
- Policy implications

**Strategic Implications:**
- CRA selective in GAAR applications
- Focus on clear cases rather than marginal planning
- Post-*Deans Knight*, bar is lower for GAAR application

## GAAR vs. Specific Anti-Avoidance Rules

**Key Principle from *Deans Knight*:**
GAAR can supplement specific anti-avoidance rules even when taxpayers technically comply

**Example:**
- Section 111(5) has specific de jure control test
- *Deans Knight* complied with de jure test
- GAAR still applied because functional equivalent achieved

**Application to Family Trusts:**
- Subsection 75(2) has specific attribution tests
- Technical compliance may not be sufficient
- If structure achieves "functional equivalent" of income attribution, GAAR could apply

## Practical Risk Mitigation

**For New Structures:**
1. Document genuine non-tax purposes extensively
2. Ensure beneficiaries have real beneficial interests
3. Use independent professional trustees
4. Avoid circular transactions
5. Maintain arm's length dealing
6. Consider mandatory disclosure if planning aggressive

**For Existing Structures:**
1. Review documentation of business purposes
2. Strengthen beneficiary involvement evidence
3. Ensure ongoing commercial substance
4. Prepare defensive memo anticipating GAAR challenge
5. Consider voluntary disclosure if non-compliant

**Red Line (Do Not Cross):**
- Accommodation parties with no real interest
- Circular transactions with no business purpose
- Structure existing solely for tax benefits
- Functional equivalent of prohibited income attribution
- Attempts to multiply single economic gain
